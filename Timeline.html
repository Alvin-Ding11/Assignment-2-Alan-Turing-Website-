Year Event
1912 Alan Turing is born in London, England with as the younger brother in an upper middle class family. His father (who worked as a clergyman) did civil service in India during Turings early childhood, leaving him to be taken care of by a retired army couple (presumebly family friends)
1926 He is enrolled into Sherborne School (a boarding school) after his teachers and parents start to take notice of his abilities. His preferance for the sciences and maths as opposed to the classics made him nupopular with the teachers during his time there.
1931 Turing starts his undergraduate at King's College, throughout his time there he earns himself first-class honors in maths and was elected a Fellow shortly after graduation.
1936 Turing published his paper on computation and his hypothetical devices that were reformulated from an arithmetic-based language conceived by Kurt Godel. Those devices would later be coined as "Turing Machines" and the paper would later be coined as the most influencial math paper in history.
1939 Starts his work at Bletchley Park, focusing primarily on a method of reliably decrypting the German Enigma machine. Through the use of probability and the creation of the "bombe" (device created specifically to decipher the Enigma machine), he was able to reliably decipher the Enigma machine for the Allies.
1941 Turing proposes to Joan Clarke, his colleague while working in Hut 8 at Bletchley Park. The engagement was short as Turing did not feel right considering his homosexuality, although Clarke was reportedly unfazed.
1945 After the war, he started working on the Automatic Computing Engine (ACE) which was the first design for an electroniclly dependent computer rather than plugboards. These designs were later used to create some of the first stored-program computers in 1950. He also continue to dabble in computers as well as maths and pattern formations.
1952 Turing was convicted of having a relationship with another man 20 years younger then him and was forced to take injections to change his hormonal structure which rendered him more feminine. He also lost a good amount of his governement clearances as a result.
1954 Alan Turing commit suicide (cyanide poisoning) at the age of 41, 8th June 1952. He was pardoned in 2014 and inspired the gouvernement to continue to exonerate others convicted in the same unjust manner. Some conspiracy's believing that Turing was assasinated for fear of working with communists due to his homosexuality and his holidays near the Iron Curtain.
